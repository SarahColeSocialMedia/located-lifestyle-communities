Overview & Character

Chittenango is a village in Madison County, 15 miles east of Syracuse, with a genuine and extraordinary claim to fame: it is the birthplace of Lyman Frank Baum, born here on May 15, 1856, who would go on to write "The Wonderful Wizard of Oz" and forty other books in the Oz series. The village has embraced this heritage with infectious enthusiasm -- yellow brick sidewalks run through downtown, Oz-themed business names appear on storefronts, and every June the village hosts Oz-Stravaganza, the longest-running and largest Wizard of Oz-themed festival in the world, drawing more than 20,000 visitors from across the United States and beyond.

Beyond the Oz identity, Chittenango is a genuinely appealing small village with a historic Erie Canal heritage preserved at the Chittenango Landing Canal Boat Museum, one of the most spectacular waterfalls in Central New York at Chittenango Falls State Park, and a community character built around its extraordinary literary identity. For buyers, Chittenango offers accessible prices within a short commute of Syracuse, a school district that serves the village well, and the kind of distinct local identity that most suburbs would envy.

Literary History

L. Frank Baum -- Born in Chittenango, May 15, 1856

Lyman Frank Baum grew up in Chittenango, the son of Benjamin Ward Baum, a successful businessman involved in oil, lumber, and a local opera house. The village's rural, picturesque character -- surrounded by rolling hills and changing seasons -- provided the foundational experiences that would later fuel one of the most enduring works of American imagination. Baum's father also had a connection to Matilda Joslyn Gage, the Fayetteville suffragist -- Baum married Gage's daughter Maud, making these two Central New York communities permanently linked through both history and family.

Baum published "The Wonderful Wizard of Oz" in 1900. The 1939 MGM film starring Judy Garland turned it into one of the most beloved stories in American cultural history. The book has never been out of print. Chittenango has honored its native son every year since 1978, when librarian Clara Houck organized the very first celebration -- a small group of children in Oz costumes parading around the library parking lot and singing Happy Birthday to Frank Baum. That event has grown into a festival that draws visitors from around the world.

The Vibe

Pace of Life

Small Village, Big Identity

Chittenango moves at a village pace -- unhurried, neighborly, and grounded in a sense of place that its extraordinary literary heritage reinforces rather than disrupts. The Oz identity is worn with genuine pride rather than forced commercialism. Residents describe gracious, friendly neighbors and a community that takes its history seriously while keeping it fun. A place that knows exactly what it is.

Downtown

Genesee Street -- Yellow Brick Sidewalks

Genesee Street (Route 5) is the spine of the village -- Oz-themed storefronts, yellow brick sidewalks, the All Things Oz Museum, local restaurants, and the Sullivan Free Library all within a compact and walkable stretch. During Oz-Stravaganza the street closes entirely for the parade. Year-round, it has the character of a village main street that has found its own identity and committed to it fully.

Who Lives Here

Families, Long-Term Residents & Value-Focused Buyers

Chittenango draws families attracted by the school district and the accessible prices, long-term residents with deep roots in the community, and buyers from the Syracuse area who want small-town character within commuting distance. The village's connection to Cazenovia Lake and the Erie Canal heritage adds outdoor and historical dimensions that appeal to buyers who want more than a standard suburb.

Commute

15-20 Minutes to Syracuse

Route 5 and Route 173 connect Chittenango to Syracuse in about 15 to 20 minutes. The I-90 Thruway is accessible nearby for longer-distance travel east toward Utica or west toward Syracuse and Rochester. The village sits at the eastern border of Onondaga County and Madison County, making it a practical base for commuters working anywhere in the greater Syracuse area.

K

What It's Known For

01

Oz-Stravaganza -- The World's Largest Wizard of Oz Festival

Held the first weekend of June every year since 1978 -- the longest-running and largest Wizard of Oz themed festival in the world. More than 20,000 visitors from across the United States and internationally descend on the village for three days of costume contests, author and illustrator signings, amusement rides, festival food, live music, fireworks, and the main parade down Genesee Street. The 2026 festival will be the 49th. Completely free to attend. Run entirely by volunteers through the International L. Frank Baum and All Things Oz Historical Foundation. One of the most genuinely joyful community events in all of Central New York.

02

All Things Oz Museum

Located on Genesee Street in a former storefront converted into a museum and gift shop -- over 15,000 Baum and Oz-related items in the collection, spanning the original books through every film, musical, and cultural adaptation of the Oz universe. Self-guided tours available; guided tours strongly recommended for the depth of knowledge the volunteer staff brings. In 2025 the museum acquired a first-edition copy of "The Wonderful Wizard of Oz" donated by one of Baum's great-grandsons. A genuine literary landmark.

03

Yellow Brick Sidewalks

The sidewalks of downtown Chittenango are painted to resemble the yellow brick road from Baum's stories -- one of those small details that transforms a village walk into something memorable. Oz-themed business names appear throughout the commercial district -- local shops and storefronts that lean into the village's heritage with an enthusiasm that feels earned rather than manufactured. A village that knows its story and tells it at every turn.

04

Chittenango Falls State Park

A 167-foot waterfall -- one of the tallest in Central New York -- plunging through a dramatic gorge just minutes from the village center. Hiking trails wind through the gorge alongside the creek, with viewing platforms positioned for the best perspectives on the falls. A genuinely spectacular natural feature that gives Chittenango a significant outdoor amenity beyond its literary identity. Open year-round; stunning in every season, but particularly dramatic in spring snowmelt and early fall.

05

Chittenango Landing Canal Boat Museum

A unique outdoor museum preserving the site of an original Erie Canal dry dock where wooden canal boats were built and repaired in the 19th century. An extraordinary piece of industrial heritage -- the kind of history that is genuinely rare and well-preserved, with ongoing archaeological excavation, restored structures, and programming that connects the Erie Canal's transformative role in American history to this specific place on the water. Free admission. Open seasonally.

06

The Wild Animal Park

A working exotic animal facility near Chittenango -- lions, tigers, and bears, quite literally -- that echoes Oz in the most concrete possible way. A unique regional attraction that gives families with children an extraordinary experience just minutes from the village. As Madison County tourism puts it: "Take a walk on the wild side" -- and in Chittenango, that is not merely a metaphor.

07

YBR Casino & Sports Book

The Yellow Brick Road Casino -- named with appropriate Chittenango flair -- operates in the area, adding a gaming and entertainment option for residents and visitors beyond the village's cultural and natural attractions. A regional entertainment draw that reflects Madison County's broader amenity base.

O

Outdoor & Recreation

--

Chittenango Falls State Park

A 167-foot waterfall in a dramatic gorge setting just minutes from the village -- hiking trails, viewing platforms, picnic areas, and one of the most impressive natural features in Central New York. Spectacular in every season and a defining outdoor amenity for Chittenango residents and visitors alike.

--

Chittenango Landing Canal Boat Museum -- Erie Canal Access

The museum site provides access to the Erie Canal corridor -- the Old Erie Canal State Historic Park's 36-mile recreational trail passes through the area, providing hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing through one of the most historically significant corridors in American history.

--

Cazenovia Lake -- 10 Minutes

The 4-mile glacial lake in neighboring Cazenovia is a 10-minute drive south -- offering swimming, boating, kayaking, and fishing in one of the most beautiful lake settings in Central New York. A significant recreational resource easily accessible from Chittenango.

--

Green Lakes State Park -- 20 Minutes

The two meromictic glacial lakes, hiking trails, golf course, and swimming beach of Green Lakes State Park are about 20 minutes west -- the broader Central New York outdoor amenity base is well within reach of Chittenango residents.

Ed

Schools

B+

Chittenango Central School District

The Chittenango Central School District serves the village and surrounding area -- a solid, community-focused district with a strong identity shaped in part by the village's cultural heritage. The district includes Chittenango High School, where school pride runs deep and the connection to the village's literary legacy is reflected in community programming and events. For families seeking an affordable entry into a genuine village community with a strong school district within commuting distance of Syracuse, Chittenango CSD represents a compelling combination. The district is also connected to the broader Cazenovia-area educational community -- some families in the Chittenango area have options depending on their specific address.

Real Estate Overview

Chittenango offers some of the most accessible home prices in the greater Syracuse area -- a genuine village with an extraordinary identity, good schools, and a short commute, at price points that reflect its distance from the most in-demand FM district communities. The housing stock is a mix of older village homes, mid-century ranches, and newer construction in the surrounding area.
