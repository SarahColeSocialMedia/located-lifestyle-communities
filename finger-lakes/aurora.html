Overview & Character

Aurora -- sometimes called Aurora-on-Cayuga -- sits on the eastern shore of Cayuga Lake in one of the most quietly extraordinary settings in the Finger Lakes. The entire village is listed on the National Register of Historic Places, with over 50 contributing properties spanning Federal, Greek Revival, Italianate, and Victorian architecture dating from the 1790s to the present. It is, quite literally, a living historic district -- not a restored attraction, but a genuine working community where people actually live among these remarkable buildings.

The village's modern identity is inseparable from two names: Henry Wells, who founded Wells College here in 1868 (the same Henry Wells who founded Wells Fargo and American Express), and Pleasant Rowland, the Wells alumna who created the American Girl doll brand, sold it to Mattel for $700 million, and poured her philanthropic resources into restoring Aurora's historic buildings and founding the Inns of Aurora -- a collection of boutique lakefront inns and restaurants that has become the village's economic and cultural anchor.

Important Note for Buyers -- 2024

Wells College announced its closure in 2024 after 156 years of operation, due to financial challenges. This is a significant change for Aurora -- the college was historically the village's largest employer and a major source of cultural activity. The Inns of Aurora is now the primary economic anchor, alongside MacKenzie-Childs. The village's long-term evolution is worth discussing with a local Realtor who understands the current landscape.

"The village is surrounded by farmland and is definitely rural living with many city advantages."

For buyers, Aurora offers something genuinely rare: an entire National Historic District as your neighborhood, with Cayuga Lake at the end of the street, the Inns of Aurora and MacKenzie-Childs as cultural anchors, and the Cayuga Lake Wine Trail at the doorstep. It's a village for buyers who prioritize refined beauty, historic character, and lakeside tranquility -- and who understand that a community in transition can also be a community of opportunity.

The Vibe

Character

Refined, Historic & Deeply Quiet

Aurora is one of the most genuinely beautiful and serene villages in New York State. It rewards slow living -- morning walks past Federal-era homes, evenings on the lake, dinners at the Aurora Inn, and the peculiar luxury of living inside a perfectly preserved 19th-century streetscape that happens to also have broadband and good wine nearby.

The Inns Effect

Boutique Resort Culture in a Village Setting

The Inns of Aurora have transformed the village into a destination resort community -- the spa, the restaurant, the lakefront properties, and the events calendar attract visitors from across the region and beyond. For residents, this means excellent dining and cultural programming right outside the door, in a village that might otherwise be entirely quiet.

Who Lives Here

Retirees, Remote Workers & History Lovers

Aurora draws a mix of retirees seeking refined lakeside living, remote workers attracted by beauty and the Ithaca commute (30 minutes south), and people who specifically sought out a National Historic District as their address. Many residents commute to Ithaca, Auburn, or Geneva for work. It's a community of intentional choices.

Honest Note

Very Small, Transitioning, Higher Taxes

Aurora has a population of around 700 and essentially no daily retail -- no grocery store, no pharmacy. Auburn (20 minutes north) handles everyday needs. Property taxes are notably high for Cayuga County. And with Wells College closed, the village is navigating a meaningful transition. All of this is worth weighing carefully alongside its extraordinary character.

What It's Known For

The American Girl Connection

Pleasant Rowland graduated from Wells College in 1962, went on to create the American Girl doll brand in 1986, and sold it to Mattel in 1998 for $700 million. She used a significant portion of that wealth to restore Aurora's historic buildings -- including purchasing and lovingly renovating the Aurora Inn, the E.B. Morgan House, and multiple other village properties -- and founded the Aurora Foundation to support ongoing preservation and the college. Without Rowland, much of Aurora's current character might not exist.

Aurora Village-Wells College Historic District

Listed on the National Register of Historic Places since 1980, the historic district encompasses over 50 contributing properties -- Federal, Greek Revival, Italianate, and Victorian architecture dating from the 1790s. The entire village business district is preserved. Walking through Aurora is unlike walking through any other Finger Lakes community -- it's a fully intact 19th-century streetscape that happens to still be alive.

Inns of Aurora

The Inns of Aurora is a collection of boutique lakefront inns -- the Aurora Inn (built 1833), the E.B. Morgan House, the Rowland House, and others -- along with a full-service spa, the 1883 Kitchen & Bar, and the Fargo Bar & Grill. Named one of the country's finest boutique resort experiences. The economic and cultural anchor of the village since Wells College's closure.

MacKenzie-Childs

MacKenzie-Childs -- the renowned hand-painted pottery and home décor company known for its whimsical Courtly Check signature design -- is headquartered just north of the village on a 65-acre Victorian farmhouse estate overlooking Cayuga Lake. Free guided tours through 15 rooms on three floors. The shop, gardens, and farmhouse are a destination in themselves, drawing design enthusiasts from across the country.

Wells College -- Founded by Henry Wells (1868-2024)

Founded by Henry Wells -- the same Henry Wells who founded Wells Fargo and American Express -- as a college for women in 1868, Wells College shaped Aurora's identity for 156 years. The scenic 300-acre campus overlooking Cayuga Lake includes a nine-hole golf course and public swimming access in summer. The campus's future is an important open question for the village as of 2024.

Morgan Opera House

A beautifully restored Victorian performance venue in the village -- still housing its original raked stage, pressed tin proscenium, leaded glass windows, and superb acoustics. A cultural gem that reflects Aurora's deep investment in preservation and the arts, hosting performances and community events year-round.

Presidential & Historical Connections

Aurora has a remarkable concentration of historical figures: President Millard Fillmore attended Cayuga Lake Academy here; Frances Folsom Cleveland (First Lady) was a Wells College alumna; Edwin B. Morgan, a founder of the New York Times, lived here; and Lewis Henry Morgan, the pioneering anthropologist, also called Aurora home.

Restaurants & Dining

Aurora's dining scene punches remarkably above its weight for a village of 700 -- entirely because of the Inns of Aurora, which operate multiple dining venues of genuine quality. Outside the Inns, options are limited but characterful.

1883 Kitchen & Bar -- Inns of Aurora

The flagship dining experience of the Inns of Aurora -- a commitment to locally sourced, scratch-made cooking where respect for farmers and ingredients is the foundation. Executive Chef Eric Lamphere produces some of the finest farm-to-table cuisine in the Finger Lakes. Reservations recommended.

The Fargo Bar & Grill

Directly across Main Street from the Aurora Inn -- casual food and atmosphere, made fresh daily from scratch with desserts by the Aurora Inn's pastry chef. Claims to serve "the best burger in the Finger Lakes" and has devoted local support. Open year-round for lunch and dinner.

Aurora Inn Market

Immediately adjacent to the Aurora Inn -- a quaint market offering Gimme! Coffee (from Ithaca), fresh produce from Long Point Orchard, and seasonal specialties from Grisamore Farms. Pastries, homemade soups, and market meals prepared daily by Aurora Inn chefs. A genuine community amenity.

Local Craft Brewery (nearby)

A nanobrewery perched on a hilltop overlooking Cayuga Lake just south of the village -- producing small batches of hoppy beers, fruited and non-fruited sours, and unusual concoctions. A beloved local option for craft beer enthusiasts.

Cayuga Lake Wine Trail Wineries

Hearts & Hands Winery -- the closest to Aurora, run by a husband and wife team on the eastern Cayuga shore -- is a short drive south. The full Cayuga Lake Wine Trail extends both north and south with dozens of producers within easy reach.

Shops & Local Businesses

Aurora's commercial life is deliberately small -- a few boutiques, an antique shop, and the extraordinary MacKenzie-Childs just north of the village. The Inns of Aurora operate the village's most active retail. Auburn (20 minutes north) handles everyday needs.

Design & Home

MacKenzie-Childs

The iconic hand-painted ceramics and home furnishings company, headquartered on a Victorian farmhouse estate just north of the village. Free guided tours daily; extraordinary shop and gardens. A destination for design lovers worldwide.

Boutique

Main Street Boutiques

A small collection of independently-owned boutiques and specialty shops along Main Street -- reflecting the village's refined character and catering to Inns of Aurora visitors and residents alike.

History

Aurora Historical Society

A small local history museum preserving and sharing Aurora's extraordinary past -- Presidential connections, women's education history, and the full story of the village's remarkable revival.

Nearby

Aurora Free Library -- Union Springs

The nearest public library is in Union Springs, about 10 miles north. The Wells College campus library closed with the college in 2024. Aurora's community programming has historically centered on Wells College and the Inns of Aurora.

Wine

Hearts & Hands Winery

Award-winning dry Riesling from a 27-acre family winery on the east shore of Cayuga Lake -- a short drive south of Aurora along scenic Route 90.

Groceries

Auburn (20 min north)

Wegmans and full retail services are available in Auburn -- a 20-minute drive north along Cayuga Lake. Planning ahead is part of Aurora village life.

Outdoor & Recreation

Cayuga Lake -- Swimming, Boating & Kayaking

The longest of the Finger Lakes at 61 miles, Cayuga Lake is right at Aurora's doorstep. The Wells College swimming area is open to the public in summer (with lifeguard). Long Point State Park is just south of the village with additional lake access, picnicking, and nature trails.

Robert Trent Jones Golf Course -- Wells College Campus

A nine-hole Robert Trent Jones-designed golf course on the Wells College campus -- available for public play and one of the most scenically situated courses in the Finger Lakes, overlooking Cayuga Lake from the hillside campus.

Cayuga Lake Wine Trail

The Cayuga Lake Wine Trail -- the nation's first wine trail -- runs along both shores of the lake with Aurora positioned on the eastern shore. Hearts & Hands is the nearest winery; dozens more are accessible by a short drive north or south along scenic Route 90.

Wells College Campus Walks

The 300-acre Wells College campus is one of the most beautiful lakeside academic campuses in New York -- open for walking, exploring the historic buildings, and taking in the Cayuga Lake views from the hillside grounds.

Long Point State Park

Just south of the village -- a beautiful state park on Cayuga Lake with swimming, picnicking, fishing, and nature trails. A quiet, accessible outdoor escape for Aurora residents year-round.

Montezuma National Wildlife Refuge (25 min north)

One of the East Coast's most important migratory bird stopover sites -- 9,000 acres of wetlands along the Atlantic Flyway, accessible from Aurora in under 30 minutes north along the lake.

Schools

B+

Southern Cayuga Central School District

Aurora is served by the Southern Cayuga Central School District -- a small rural district with the intimate class sizes and community character that come with genuine small-town schooling. The district has historically benefited from the cultural enrichment of Wells College, which hosted public lectures, performances, and screenings. Families prioritizing highly-rated academic rankings will want to compare surrounding districts; families valuing a small school experience in an extraordinarily beautiful community will find the setting itself is part of the education.

Real Estate Overview

Aurora's real estate market reflects its extraordinary character -- median home values around $411,000 as of early 2025, in a village where every home is part of a National Historic District. The housing stock spans Federal, Greek Revival, Italianate, and Victorian homes from the 1790s onward, with lakefront properties commanding significant premiums. Property taxes are notably high for Cayuga County -- the effective rate is roughly 1.94%, well above the national median. Buyers should factor this into long-term cost calculations. The small inventory and highly desirable setting mean Aurora properties rarely stay on the market long when they do appear.

Village Historic Homes

Federal to Victorian -- All in a Historic District

Every home in Aurora's village core is part of the National Historic District -- a rare distinction. Federal, Greek Revival, Italianate, and Victorian homes in various states of loving preservation. Small inventory, high desirability.

Cayuga Lakefront

Premium for Water Access & Views

Properties with direct Cayuga Lake frontage or views command significant premiums. The Inns of Aurora properties represent the top of the market; private lakefront homes are rare and highly sought after when they appear.
