Overview

Honeoye (pronounced HUN-ee-oy) is a hamlet at the north end of Honeoye Lake in the Town of Richmond, Ontario County. It is one of the quieter communities in the Finger Lakes -- a mix of agricultural families, seasonal lake residents, local artists drawn by the scenery, and year-rounders who value the unhurried pace above all else. Honeoye Lake itself is the shallowest of all eleven Finger Lakes at just 30 feet -- which means it freezes reliably early each winter, making it one of the best ice fishing lakes in western New York, and warms quickly in summer for swimming and boating.

The hamlet has a small but genuine downtown along Route 20A -- gas stations, a grocery, restaurants, hardware, a doctor and dentist, and the Honeoye Public Library, which hosts free Friday night concerts at the community gazebo through the summer. Bristol Mountain ski resort is 15 minutes away. The hamlet has a long connection to the arts, with internationally known sculptor Philippe Faraut based here and his work visible around town.

A remarkable piece of local history: in 1926, the City of Rochester announced plans to acquire Honeoye Lake as a drinking water reservoir and submerge the entire hamlet. After years of litigation, Rochester decided in 1935 to obtain water from Lake Ontario instead -- and Honeoye Lake's character as a recreational community was immediately and permanently sealed.

What's Here
--

Ring of Fire -- July 3rd & Labor Day Weekend

One of the great Finger Lakes traditions: residents around the entire shoreline of Honeoye Lake light flares simultaneously on the night before the Fourth of July and again the Saturday before Labor Day to mark the end of summer. The effect from the water -- the entire lake rimmed in fire -- is genuinely spectacular and a defining community ritual.

--

Sandy Bottom Park

At the north end of Honeoye Lake -- a sandy beach, boardwalk, picnic pavilion, playground, and softball field with swimming and small boat access. The primary public lake amenity for residents and visitors alike.

--

Harriet Hollister Spencer State Recreation Area

Located in the Town of Canadice on the ridge above Honeoye Lake -- 1,550 acres of hiking, mountain biking, snowmobiling, cross-country skiing, and snowshoeing, with a stunning panoramic view of the lake and, on clear days, the Rochester skyline.

--

Bristol Mountain -- 15 Minutes

One of the Finger Lakes' premier ski resorts is just 15 minutes from Honeoye -- making this hamlet one of the best-positioned communities in Ontario County for winter sports access.

--

Local Arts Scene

International sculptor Philippe Faraut is based in Honeoye and his work can be seen around town and inside the Mill Creek Cafe. The hamlet has a history of attracting artists to its landscape and community character.

--

Ice Fishing

Because Honeoye Lake is the shallowest Finger Lake, it freezes more reliably and earlier than its neighbors -- making it a premier ice fishing destination for walleye, perch, crappie, and bass. When the fishermen are out on the ice, locals know the conditions are right.

Real Estate

Honeoye Lake properties include a mix of seasonal cottages, year-round lakefront homes, and rural farmhouses in the surrounding hills. Lakefront commands a premium; in-hamlet homes are among the more accessible in Ontario County wine country.
