Communities by Lake

Conesus Lake
8 miles · Livingston County · Westernmost Finger Lake
Hamlet · North End
Lakeville
The lake's primary commerce center. Vitale Park Sunday concerts, excellent fishing, and Letchworth State Park 20 minutes west.
→
Hemlock Lake
7 miles · Livingston / Ontario Counties · Forever Wild
Hemlock Lake is forever wild. Rochester's primary drinking water source since 1876. All shoreline development was cleared beginning in 1895. Transferred to NYS DEC in 2010 and pledged forever wild. Wilderness paddling and fishing only (10HP max). No communities on Hemlock Lake.
Canadice Lake
3 miles · Ontario County · Forever Wild · Smallest Finger Lake
Canadice Lake is forever wild. The smallest and highest-elevation Finger Lake. Transferred to NYS DEC in 2010 with Hemlock for $13.7 million -- both pledged forever wild. Surrounded by Hemlock-Canadice State Forest with 14 miles of hiking trails. No communities on Canadice Lake.
Honeoye Lake
4.5 miles · Ontario County · Shallowest Finger Lake
Hamlet · North End
Honeoye
Ring of Fire on the Fourth of July. Sandy Bottom Park. Bristol Mountain 15 minutes away. Freezes first -- the best ice fishing lake in the western Finger Lakes.
→
Canandaigua Lake
15.5 miles · Ontario County
City · North End
Canandaigua
The Lady of the Lake. Sonnenberg Gardens, Bristol Mountain skiing, and a revitalized lakefront city at the northern end.
→
Village · South End
Naples
Grape Pie Capital of the World. Purple fire hydrants. 30,000-40,000 visitors to a village of 1,000 each September for the Grape Festival.
→
Keuka Lake
19.6 miles · Yates / Steuben Counties · The only Y-shaped Finger Lake
Village · Northeast Fork
Penn Yan
The heart of wine country. The Windmill Market, Keuka Outlet Trail, Mennonite farm culture, and three lakes within easy reach.
→
Village · South End
Hammondsport
Cradle of Aviation. Glenn Curtiss, the oldest winery in New York, Pulteney Square, and America's Coolest Small Town.
→
Seneca Lake
38 miles · Seneca / Schuyler Counties · Largest by Area · Deepest at 618 ft
City · North End
Geneva
Hobart and William Smith Colleges, the Smith Opera House, Victorian South Main Street, and the gateway to the Seneca Lake Wine Trail.
→
Village · South End
Watkins Glen
The gorge. The racetrack. Five USA Today number-one awards. One of the most dramatically beautiful villages in the Finger Lakes.
→
Cayuga Lake
38 miles · Cayuga / Tompkins / Seneca Counties · Longest Finger Lake
Town · North End
Seneca Falls
Birthplace of the Women's Rights movement. The Cayuga-Seneca Canal. Possibly the inspiration for Bedford Falls in It's a Wonderful Life.
→
Village · East Shore
Aurora
Entire village on the National Register. Inns of Aurora, MacKenzie-Childs, the American Girl story, and Cayuga Lake at every doorstep.
→
City · South End
Ithaca
Cornell University, gorges, waterfalls, and the most vibrant small city in the Finger Lakes. Named CNN's Best Small Town in America.
→
Owasco Lake
11 miles · Cayuga County
City · North End
Auburn
History's Hometown. Harriet Tubman's chosen city for 50 years. The Seward House, sound-on-film invention, Prison City Brewing, and Emerson Park on the lake.
→
Skaneateles Lake
15 miles · Onondaga County · Purest water in the Finger Lakes
Village · North End
Skaneateles
The jewel of the Finger Lakes. The Sherwood Inn, Clift Park on the lake, sightseeing cruises, and water so pure the village drinks it from the tap.
→
Otisco Lake
5.4 miles · Onondaga County · Easternmost Finger Lake
Hamlets · Marietta & Amber
Marietta & Amber
Two lakes in one, divided by a causeway. World-record tiger muskie fishing. Year-round lake homes 15 minutes from Syracuse.
