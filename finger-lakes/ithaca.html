Overview & Character

Ithaca sits at the southern tip of Cayuga Lake — the longest of the Finger Lakes — in a dramatic landscape of gorges, waterfalls, and forested hillsides that give the city its most famous tagline: "Ithaca is Gorges." With over 100 waterfalls within city limits and two major universities anchoring its economy and culture, Ithaca is unlike anywhere else in New York State — a place where Ivy League intellect meets outdoor adventure, and where the food at the farmers market rivals anything you'd find in Brooklyn.

Cornell University, founded in 1865 and perched dramatically on East Hill above the city, is one of the world's great research universities and sets the intellectual tone for everything here. Ithaca College adds to that critical mass from its South Hill campus. Together, the two schools bring in students, faculty, and researchers from around the world — giving Ithaca a cosmopolitan, globally minded energy that's rare for a city of 32,000.

"Ithaca is a Finger Lakes destination in and of itself, blending hippie grunge with the buttoned-up Ivy League atmosphere of Cornell University."

— Lonely Planet

Named CNN's Best Town to Visit in America for 2025, Ithaca draws visitors and new residents alike for its natural beauty, vibrant food scene, progressive culture, and quality of life that punches well above its weight for a small city. It is also, by some measures, the second most expensive city to live in New York State after New York City — a reflection of low housing supply and persistently high demand driven by the universities.

The Vibe

Culture & Politics

Deeply Progressive & Proudly Diverse

Ithaca is one of the most liberal cities in New York State — a place where sustainability, social justice, LGBTQ+ inclusivity, and community activism are woven into daily life. One of the first cities in the US to prohibit discrimination based on sexual orientation, it has a long history of progressive firsts.

Who Lives Here

Students, Professors, Creatives & Lifers

The population is a mix of Cornell and Ithaca College students and faculty, longtime residents who came for school and never left, artists, farmers, musicians, and professionals drawn by the quality of life. The city has a high renter population due to its transient academic cycle, but also a strong core of committed permanent residents.

Pace of Life

Energetic, Walkable & Nature-Saturated

Ithaca is remarkably walkable and bikeable for an upstate city — electric bikeshare bikes are everywhere, The Commons pedestrian mall is the social hub, and the gorge trails are right in the middle of town. Life here moves with purpose but never feels rushed. The natural world is always minutes away.

Honest Note

Hilly, Snowy & Sometimes Expensive

Ithaca's terrain is dramatically hilly — expect to work for your views. Winters are cold and snowy, and the hilly roads require snow tires. Housing costs are high relative to the rest of upstate New York. And while crime is not a major issue, it is slightly higher than comparable small cities. Eyes open, and most residents wouldn't trade it.

What It's Known For

🌊

Over 100 Waterfalls — Including Taughannock Falls

Ithaca has more waterfalls per square mile than almost anywhere in the eastern United States. Taughannock Falls — at 215 feet — is three stories taller than Niagara Falls and the tallest single-drop waterfall east of the Rocky Mountains. Gorge trails weave through the city, with Cascadilla Gorge connecting downtown to Cornell University through a natural stone canyon.

🎓

Cornell University

One of the world's great Ivy League research universities, founded in 1865 on East Hill. Cornell's campus is a destination in its own right — the Cornell Botanic Gardens, the Johnson Museum of Art, and the Lab of Ornithology (the world's foremost bird research center) are all open to the public and anchor Ithaca's intellectual identity.

🥗

Moosewood Restaurant

One of the most influential restaurants in American culinary history. Opened in 1973, Moosewood helped pioneer vegetarian cuisine in the United States and its bestselling cookbook changed how the country thought about plant-based food. It's still serving on Cayuga Street, five decades later.

🛒

Ithaca Farmers Market

One of the finest farmers markets in New York State — held every Wednesday and Saturday at the Steamboat Landing pavilion on Cayuga Lake. Local produce, artisan foods, crafts, live music, and prepared food from dozens of vendors. A cornerstone of Ithaca community life year-round.

🎵

Live Music & Arts Scene

The historic State Theatre of Ithaca hosts nationally touring acts September through May. The Hangar Theatre is a beloved regional company. Street musicians fill The Commons year-round. For a city its size, Ithaca's cultural output — in music, film, art, and theater — is extraordinary.

🍺

Craft Beverage Scene

The Cayuga Lake Wine Trail — the nation's first wine trail — begins right at Ithaca's doorstep. Add South Hill Cider, Ithaca Beer Co., and a growing number of craft breweries and distilleries, and Ithaca is a serious destination for beverage lovers.

🗺️

Neighborhoods to Know

Ithaca's neighborhoods each have a distinct character — shaped by proximity to the universities, the gorges, and Cayuga Lake.

Most Walkable
Downtown & The Commons

The four-block pedestrian mall is the heart of the city — 100+ independent shops, restaurants, galleries, and street performers. The social and commercial hub of Ithaca.

Most Sought-After
Fall Creek

A fan favorite among buyers — classic architecture, community gardens, proximity to the gorge and Sciencenter, and a strong neighborhood identity. Homes move fast here.

Most Prestigious
Cayuga Heights

A separate village adjacent to Cornell with stately historic homes, excellent schools, tree-lined streets, and the highest home values in the Ithaca area. Park-like and quiet.

Near Cornell
Cornell Heights

Historic homes along Fall Creek Gorge, close to Cornell campus. Diverse architectural styles, strong community feel, and scenic trails right outside the door.

Near Ithaca College
South Hill

Ithaca College's campus offers some of the best views of Cayuga Lake in the city. South Hill has a more suburban feel with access to Buttermilk Falls State Park.

Family-Friendly
Belle Sherman

A quiet, walkable neighborhood near Collegetown known for its safety, top-rated elementary school, community parks, and easy commute to Cornell and downtown.

Restaurants & Dining

Ithaca's dining scene is far beyond what you'd expect from a city its size — driven by Cornell's hospitality school, a globally diverse population, and deep roots in local and sustainable food culture.

🥗

Moosewood Restaurant

An American culinary landmark since 1973 — the restaurant that helped pioneer vegetarian cuisine nationally. Still serving on Cayuga Street, with seasonal vegetable-forward dishes and the warmth of a true community institution.

🥐

Le Café Cent Dix

Contemporary French bistro fare with a strong emphasis on seasonal, local vegetables — named one of CNN's highlights for Ithaca in 2025. Duck breast, steak frites, and rotating seasonal vegetable dishes in an intimate setting.

🍹

Mercato Bar & Kitchen

One of downtown Ithaca's most popular dinner spots — a lively bar and kitchen on The Commons known for creative cocktails and a menu that draws on global influences.

☕

Gimme! Coffee

An Ithaca institution with multiple locations — a specialty roaster and café that has been a cornerstone of the city's coffee culture since 2000. Also found in Cayuga Heights and New York City.

🌮

Luna Inspired Street Food

Global street food and American dishes in the heart of downtown — a lively, casual spot known for mac and cheese, burgers, and street tacos that draws a devoted local following.

🍦

Purity Ice Cream

A beloved Ithaca institution since 1936 — locally owned and coined "Ice Cream of The Finger Lakes." A summer pilgrimage for locals and visitors alike.

🍎

South Hill Cider

A scenic cidery near Buttermilk Falls offering guided tastings of fermented ciders, drinks by the glass, and live music — with campsites bookable online. A true Ithaca original.

🛍️

Shops & Local Businesses

The Ithaca Commons — a four-block pedestrian mall in the heart of downtown — is home to over 100 independently owned shops, galleries, and eateries. Ithaca has a strong local-first shopping culture and minimal chain retail presence downtown.

Books

Autumn Leaves Used Books

A beloved independent used bookstore on The Commons — a perfect rainy afternoon stop with an eclectic, well-curated selection.

Groceries

GreenStar Food Co-op

A worker- and community-owned natural foods co-op with two locations — the heart of Ithaca's local and organic food culture. A beloved institution.

Wine

Cellar d'Or

A curated wine shop on The Commons specializing in Finger Lakes wines alongside international selections — a great stop before any dinner party.

Organic Home

Home Green Home

Organic products for the home — part of The Commons' diverse retail mix reflecting Ithaca's sustainability-minded community values.

Boutique

The Commons Boutiques

Dozens of independently owned clothing boutiques, gift shops, and specialty stores line The Commons — strongly independent, with minimal chain presence.

Groceries

Wegmans & Aldi

Full-service Wegmans and Aldi both located off NY Route 13 — accessible for full grocery runs outside of the co-op.

Outdoor & Recreation

Outdoor recreation is inseparable from life in Ithaca. The gorges, waterfalls, lake, and state parks are not weekend destinations — they're part of the daily commute, the lunch break, and the evening walk.

🌊

Taughannock Falls State Park

Home to the tallest single-drop waterfall east of the Rocky Mountains at 215 feet — three stories taller than Niagara. Open year-round, with two main viewing access points and hiking trails through the dramatic gorge. A short drive north of the city.

🥾

Cascadilla Gorge Trail

A secluded cascading falls and stone path connecting downtown Ithaca directly to Cornell University through a natural gorge — a quiet, beautiful refuge that runs right through the heart of the city. Open seasonally.

💦

Buttermilk Falls & Robert H. Treman State Parks

Two beloved state parks on the south side of the city — winding gorges, multiple waterfalls including the notable Lucifer Falls, swimming holes, camping, and miles of trails. Robert H. Treman is one of the most dramatic parks in the entire Finger Lakes region.

🚲

Black Diamond Trail & Cayuga Waterfront Trail

The Black Diamond Trail is an 8.5-mile rail trail connecting Trumansburg to Ithaca — equally good for biking or cross-country skiing. The Cayuga Waterfront Trail runs along the lake through Cass Park, connecting to the Farmers Market pavilion.

🌊

Cayuga Lake — Swimming, Boating & Paddling

The longest of the Finger Lakes at 61 miles, Cayuga Lake offers swimming at Stewart Park and Taughannock, kayaking, paddleboarding, sailing, and access to the Cayuga Lake Wine Trail along its eastern and western shores.

🌿

Cornell Botanic Gardens

Free and open to the public year-round — 2,800 acres of natural areas and gardens on Cornell's campus, including arboreta, wetlands, and beautifully curated botanical collections. One of the great public gardens of the Northeast.

Schools

A

Ithaca City School District

The Ithaca City School District is well-regarded and diverse, serving the city's broad mix of longtime residents and university families. Ithaca Senior High School earns an A rating from Niche with an average SAT score of 1350 and 38% of students enrolled in advanced placement programs. Boynton Middle School earns an A- rating. The district reflects Ithaca's progressive values — with strong arts, music, and equity programs alongside rigorous academics.

Cayuga Heights — Village Schools

The village of Cayuga Heights, adjacent to Ithaca, is served by the Ithaca City School District but is considered the top-rated residential area in the broader Ithaca market — with the highest-rated schools, lowest crime, and most prestigious home values. Families prioritizing school ratings often target Cayuga Heights specifically.

Real Estate Overview

Ithaca is one of the most expensive real estate markets in upstate New York — a direct result of chronically low inventory and persistently high demand from university employees, graduates who stayed, and remote workers attracted by the quality of life. The home price-to-income ratio is 46% higher than the New York State average — a stark number that reflects the supply-demand imbalance. Village homes in Cayuga Heights represent the top of the market; more affordable options exist on the West Hill and in the city's outer neighborhoods.
