Overview

Conesus Lake is the westernmost of the eleven Finger Lakes -- long called the Jewel of Livingston County -- a year-round recreational lake 25 miles south of Rochester, off Interstate 390. Lakeville is the hamlet at the lake's north end, serving as the primary commerce center and the location of the lake's outlet, Conesus Creek, which flows to the Genesee River.

Unlike Hemlock and Canadice to the east, Conesus Lake is a heavily populated, fully developed residential lake -- the entire shoreline is served by public sewers, water, and electricity, making it one of the most livable of the smaller Finger Lakes year-round. The community is a mix of longtime lake families, Rochester commuters who make the 25-mile drive, and seasonal residents who have turned their cottages into year-round homes.

The nearest full-service community is Geneseo -- just 5 minutes south -- home to SUNY Geneseo, a Main Street with excellent restaurants and shops, and the full amenity set that Lakeville's hamlet scale doesn't offer independently. Letchworth State Park -- the Grand Canyon of the East -- is 20 minutes west.

What's Here
--

Vitale Park -- Sunday Night Concerts

The Town of Livonia's beautiful community park at the northern end of the lake, right at the outlet. Picnic areas, a gazebo, fishing access, and a beloved summer concert series held every Sunday evening. A genuine community gathering point and one of the most pleasant public spaces on any of the smaller Finger Lakes.

--

Conesus Lake Fishing

A productive year-round fishery for warmwater species -- walleye, northern pike, largemouth and smallmouth bass, yellow perch, tiger muskie, and bluegill. The lake has traditionally been one of the best bass and pike lakes in western New York. Boat launches on both the east and west shores with easy access. Ice fishing in winter when conditions allow.

--

Long Point Park -- Public Swimming

On the west shore in the town of Geneseo -- a community park on a peninsula with public swimming, picnic tables, grills, a pavilion, and a hand boat launch. One of the few spots on Conesus Lake with a guarded swimming area.

--

Chip Holt Nature Center

At the north end of Conesus Lake -- educational programs, nature trails, and recreational opportunities focused on the lake's ecology and watershed. A community resource reflecting Livingston County's investment in environmental education.

--

Geneseo -- 5 Minutes South

SUNY Geneseo, one of the most respected public liberal arts colleges in the country, anchors a thriving small-city Main Street with restaurants, shops, galleries, and the cultural energy of a college town. The nearest full-service hub for Lakeville and Conesus Lake residents.

--

Letchworth State Park -- 20 Minutes West

The Grand Canyon of the East -- 17 miles of gorge, three major waterfalls (including the 107-foot Middle Falls), and over 66 miles of trails. Consistently rated one of the best state parks in the country. An extraordinary backyard amenity for Conesus Lake residents.

Real Estate

Conesus Lake offers a mix of lakefront cottages converted to year-round homes, newly built lake houses, and more modest in-hamlet properties in Lakeville. As one of the most fully developed of the smaller Finger Lakes, the lakefront market is active and competitive. Geneseo properties -- 5 minutes south -- offer a solid alternative for buyers who want lake proximity without lakefront prices.
