Overview & Character

Naples sits in a dramatic glacially-carved valley at the southern end of Canandaigua Lake, surrounded by the steep, vine-covered slopes of the Bristol Hills. It is one of the smallest villages in this guide — barely 1,000 residents — and one of the most distinctively itself. Naples doesn't try to be anything other than what it is: a grape-growing, pie-baking, hill-climbing, wine-making community that has been celebrating its harvest culture since 1961.

The village name itself comes from a visiting European count who reportedly remarked that the scenery rivaled Naples, Italy — and the name stuck in 1808. Before European settlement it was Nundawao, an old Seneca village. The town was established in 1789 with settlers from Massachusetts, making it one of the oldest communities in the western Finger Lakes.

🍇

The Grape Pie Capital of the World — And It's Earned

In the fall of 1959, Irene Bouchard began baking Concord grape pies out of her home in Naples. Over the years she sold roughly 300,000 pies — and helped Naples earn a title that has stuck ever since. Today, an estimated 70,000 grape pies are sold in Naples each year, and the annual Grape Festival features the World's Greatest Grape Pie Contest where over 20 local bakers compete for the coveted title. Monica's Pies on Main Street is the current standard-bearer — open year-round and worth a special trip in September when the Concord grapes come in.

For buyers, Naples offers something genuinely rare — a village with a singular, authentic identity, extraordinary natural scenery, Bristol Mountain skiing nine miles away, multiple wineries within walking distance, and a professional theater company in its backyard. It's not a destination for buyers who need urban amenities. It's a destination for buyers who want to live inside one of the Finger Lakes' most beautiful and character-rich landscapes.

"Nestled in the hills at the southern end of Canandaigua Lake, Naples is renowned for its stunning natural surroundings, which have inspired many artists and farmers to create and work here."

The Vibe

Pace of Life

Deeply Seasonal & Genuinely Rural

Naples lives by the agricultural calendar — grape harvest in September brings tens of thousands of visitors to a village of 1,000, while winter is quiet, snow-covered, and centered on Bristol Mountain skiing. Spring brings the vineyards back to life; summer means Canandaigua Lake day trips and outdoor theater. Each season here has a distinct, unhurried rhythm.

Arts & Culture

Unexpectedly Rich Creative Scene

For a village this small, Naples has a remarkable arts presence — the Bristol Valley Theater brings professional productions from New York City and regional artists for a full summer season. The surrounding hills have long attracted painters, sculptors, and artisans. The Grape Festival itself is a juried arts and crafts show as much as a food celebration.

Who Lives Here

Artists, Winemakers & Nature Seekers

Naples draws a mix of multi-generational local families, winery and distillery workers, artists attracted by the scenery and the theater community, retirees seeking genuine rural seclusion, and outdoor enthusiasts who want Bristol Mountain skiing and Canandaigua Lake access within minutes of their front door.

Honest Note

Very Small, Very Rural — Intentionally So

Naples has one main thoroughfare in and out. Daily amenity options are extremely limited — a handful of local restaurants, a wayside market, and a few specialty shops. Canandaigua (16 miles north) is the nearest real hub for groceries, medical care, and broader retail. This is a feature, not a bug, for the right buyer — but eyes open.

🏛️

What It's Known For

🍇

Naples Grape Festival — Since 1961

Held every September, the Naples Grape Festival has been a Finger Lakes autumn tradition for over 60 years — drawing between 30,000 and 40,000 visitors to a village of 1,000 for two days of grape pie contests, juried arts and crafts, live music, wine and cider tastings, and every grape-flavored treat imaginable. The World's Greatest Grape Pie Contest is the centerpiece — over 20 local bakers compete for the coveted title each year.

🎭

Bristol Valley Theater

A professional regional theater company that has called Naples home for decades — bringing productions by artists from New York City and across the region to a beautifully intimate venue in the Bristol Hills. Comedy, dance, drama, and family programming through a full summer season. One of the great cultural surprises of the Finger Lakes.

⛷️

Bristol Mountain — 9 Miles Away

Bristol Mountain ski resort is just 9 miles north of Naples — one of the closest major ski areas to any Finger Lakes community. With 32 trails and 1,200 vertical feet, it's a genuine ski destination that makes Naples an exceptional choice for winter sports enthusiasts.

🍷

Bristol Hills Wine Country

The hills surrounding Naples are blanketed with vineyards — Hazlitt Red Cat Cellars (in the former Widmer Wine Cellars), Arbor Hill Winery & Grapery, and Inspire Moore Winery are all right in or near the village. The terrain and microclimate of the Bristol Hills produce distinctive wines, and the Canandaigua Lake Wine Trail extends the options north.

🔥

Purple Fire Hydrants

Naples takes its grape identity seriously — the village fire hydrants are painted purple. A charming, genuine expression of community pride that sums up Naples perfectly: unhurried, confident in its identity, and not above a little fun.

🍂

One of the Best Fall Drives in New York

The drive between Rochester and Naples is consistently cited as one of the most spectacular fall foliage drives in New York State. County Roads 12, 21, 33, 34, and 64 each offer stunning views — especially in October when the Bristol Hills turn. The Jump Off Overlook on Gannett Hill offers panoramic views where, on a clear day, you can see the Rochester city skyline.

🍽️

Restaurants & Dining

Naples' dining scene reflects the village's scale and character — a handful of beloved local spots, a distillery restaurant, a historic hotel, and the unmissable pie shops that make September here a pilgrimage. Hours are very seasonal; always check ahead.

🥧

Monica's Pies

The standard-bearer for Naples grape pie — open year-round on Main Street, Monica's is the reason many people visit Naples in the first place. The Concord grape pie in September, when the fresh harvest comes in, is a once-a-year experience not to be missed.

🥃

Hollerhorn Distilling

Globally inspired cuisine paired with house-made spirits in a beautiful setting — one of the most sophisticated dining experiences in the Naples area and a local anchor for food and beverage culture beyond the standard wine-trail format.

🏨

The Naples Hotel

A local landmark built in 1895 — originally a travelers' stop between Canandaigua and Bath, now under renewed ownership with updated rooms and a dining room that serves as a genuine village gathering place.

🍳

Old School Café

A renovated 1870s schoolhouse offering a creative brunch menu — one of the most charming dining settings in the Finger Lakes, with locally sourced ingredients and the kind of thoughtful cooking that fits Naples' artistic character.

🍕

Redwood Restaurant

A dependable local comfort food spot — burgers, pasta, and classic American fare in an unpretentious setting. The kind of neighborhood restaurant that makes everyday life in a small village genuinely pleasant.

🛒

Joseph's Wayside Market

A local market on South Main Street carrying local and regional products including grape pie, fresh local grapes in season, and a range of Finger Lakes specialty items. A community staple for daily provisions.

🛍️

Shops & Local Businesses

Main Street Naples is genuinely compact — a one-street village with a handful of specialty shops, galleries, and local businesses. What it lacks in variety it makes up for in character and authenticity.

Art

The Naples Studio

A local art gallery and studio space on Reed Street — reflecting Naples' deep connection to the visual arts and the creative community drawn by the Bristol Hills scenery.

Fishing

Sutton Company

An old-time fishing gear and tackle shop — all the equipment needed for Canandaigua Lake and surrounding waterways, run with the kind of local expertise only a multi-generational shop provides.

Art & Gifts

Cheshire Union (nearby)

Located in a historic four-room schoolhouse south of Canandaigua on Route 21 — a gallery, gift shop, artist studio space, and classroom for over 120 regional artists. A remarkable creative hub just north of Naples.

Winery

Arbor Hill Winery & Grapery

A Naples institution producing wines from Concord and vinifera grapes — and offering a unique "grapery" experience celebrating the village's deep grape culture beyond just wine.

Nature

West Hill Preserve

A 450-acre nature preserve at the south end of the Bristol Hills — an excellent spot to observe wildlife including turkeys, hawks, deer, foxes, and turtles in the hills above the village.

Groceries

Canandaigua (16 mi north)

Full grocery and retail options — including Wegmans — are 16 miles north in Canandaigua. Naples itself has basic provisions only; planning ahead is part of rural village life.

🏞️

Outdoor & Recreation

⛷️

Bristol Mountain Ski Resort — 9 Miles

The closest major ski area to Naples — 32 trails, 1,200 vertical feet, and the longest season in the Finger Lakes. Bristol also offers summer aerial adventures and zipline tours, and the spectacular Fall Sky Rides chairlift from mid-September through October for foliage viewing.

🥾

Grimes Glen County Park

A beautiful gorge park just outside the village — waterfall hiking through a dramatic rock canyon that offers a taste of Watkins Glen-style scenery right in Naples' backyard. One of the most scenic short hikes in Ontario County.

🦅

Jump Off Overlook — Gannett Hill

One of the most spectacular viewpoints in the entire Finger Lakes — the Jump Off Overlook on Gannett Hill offers panoramic views of Canandaigua Lake, Bare Hill, South Hill, and Great Hill Nature Preserve. On clear days, the Rochester city skyline is visible. A defining Naples experience.

🍷

Bristol Hills Wine Country

Multiple wineries within minutes of the village center — Hazlitt Red Cat Cellars, Arbor Hill Winery, and Inspire Moore Winery are all nearby. The Canandaigua Lake Wine Trail extends the options north with Bristol Mountain skiing as a winter complement.

⛵

Canandaigua Lake — 16 Miles North

The Sawmill Restaurant and Canandaigua Lake State Park are accessible from Naples as a day trip north — swimming, boating, and lakefront dining within easy reach of the village.

🍎

U-Pick Orchards & Farm Stands

Jerome's U-Pick and other local farms along Route 53 offer apple picking, fresh produce, and farm-direct shopping that reflects Naples' deep agricultural roots. Fall harvest season is the pinnacle — fresh Concord grapes, apples, and squash from the surrounding hillside farms.

📍

What's Nearby

16 miles north

Canandaigua

The nearest full-service hub — Wegmans, F.F. Thompson Hospital, Sonnenberg Gardens, Bristol Mountain access, and a revitalized downtown on the lake.

9 miles north

Bristol Mountain

The closest major ski resort — 32 trails, 1,200 vertical feet, aerial adventures, and spectacular fall chairlift rides. Naples' most important outdoor amenity.

1 hour north

Rochester

Greater Rochester International Airport, University of Rochester Medical Center, and the full amenities of New York's third-largest city — connected by some of the most scenic fall drives in the state.

30 minutes south

Hammondsport

The southern tip of Keuka Lake — Glenn Curtiss Aviation Museum, the Keuka Lake Wine Trail's southern producers, and a beautifully preserved historic village square.

30 minutes east

Penn Yan

The heart of Keuka Lake wine country — the Windmill Farm Market, Keuka Lake Outlet Trail, and additional wine and mead producers in a genuine small-town setting.

45 minutes south

Corning

The Corning Museum of Glass — one of the world's great museums — and a revitalized Market Street with dining and arts in a small city with genuine character.

🎓

Schools

B

Naples Central School District

A small rural district serving the Naples community — with the intimate class sizes and tight-knit school community that come with genuine small-town schooling. The district reflects Naples' character: arts are valued, the outdoors are part of the curriculum, and the school is a central community institution. Families prioritizing highly-rated academic rankings will want to consider nearby districts; families prioritizing a genuine small-school experience will find Naples delivers it fully.

Real Estate Overview

Naples offers some of the most affordable and scenically dramatic real estate in the Finger Lakes — a village of historic homes surrounded by vineyard-covered hills, with Bristol Mountain skiing minutes away and Canandaigua Lake accessible to the north. The housing stock includes 19th-century homes throughout the village, farmhouses on the surrounding hillsides, and a growing number of vacation rental properties reflecting the area's tourism appeal. For buyers seeking genuine rural seclusion with extraordinary natural beauty and wine country identity, Naples is exceptional value.
