Overview & Character

Skaneateles — pronounced "SKAN-ee-AT-ləs" — is an affluent Finger Lakes village set at the northern tip of one of the most pristine lakes in North America. Just 20 miles southwest of Syracuse, it occupies a world apart: a compact, walkable downtown of boutique shops, white-tablecloth restaurants, and Victorian homes, all anchored by a lake so clean it has supplied drinking water to the city of Syracuse for over a century without filtration.

The name is Iroquois for "long lake," and at 16 miles long, Skaneateles Lake lives up to it — stretching southeast through cliffs and pastures with a clarity that turns the water a striking blue-green. Settlement began in the 1790s, and by the 1800s Skaneateles had attracted wealthy merchants, industrialists, and members of the Roosevelt family, leaving behind a legacy of Greek Revival and Gothic Revival architecture still evident on every street.

"You feel a sense of belonging here — whether it's to raise a family, build a business, escape for a weekend, or stay for the season."

Today Skaneateles draws two distinct crowds: families who come for the school district (consistently among the best in Central New York) and second-home buyers from New York City and the Northeast. Home values are among the highest in the Finger Lakes region — and inventory is chronically thin.

The Vibe

Pace of Life

Unhurried & Intentional

Skaneateles moves at the pace of the lake. Summer brings sailboats, lake cruises, and dinner al fresco. Fall brings foliage and a quieter village locals love. Winter brings Dickens Christmas — one of New York's most beloved holiday traditions.

Who Lives Here

Professionals, Families & Second-Home Buyers

A mix of longtime locals, Syracuse-area professionals commuting 20 minutes, retirees, and weekend residents from NYC and beyond. Median household income is $114K. Homes rarely hit the market — and when they do, they sell fast.

Politics & Culture

Quietly Conservative, Community-Minded

Skaneateles trends conservative relative to broader New York State, but the village's identity is more civic than political — rallying around the school district, the lake's cleanliness, and a shared pride in keeping things beautiful.

Summer vs. Year-Round

A Town of Two Seasons

The population doubles from Memorial Day to Labor Day as seasonal residents arrive. Year-round, Skaneateles is quieter, smaller — and some say even more beautiful. The lake in October, with fall foliage reflected in the water, is unforgettable.

🏛️

What It's Known For

💧

One of the Cleanest Lakes in the Country

Skaneateles Lake has supplied unfiltered drinking water to Syracuse for over a century. Development is tightly controlled, water quality is legendary, and the lake remains the community's most fiercely protected asset.

⛵

Mid-Lakes Navigation Cruises

A Skaneateles institution — Mid-Lakes Navigation offers sightseeing, lunch, dinner, cocktail, and themed specialty cruises on the lake aboard their antique fleet. Running mid-May through mid-October, it's one of the quintessential Finger Lakes experiences.

🎄

Dickens Christmas

Each weekend from late November through December, the village transforms into a Dickensian scene — Victorian-costumed actors, carolers, and horse-drawn wagon parades. Visitors come from across New York State for it.

🏛️

Historic Architecture & Underground Railroad History

A remarkable concentration of 19th-century homes, including Gothic Revival and Greek Revival landmarks. James Canning Fuller operated an active Underground Railroad station here in the 1840s.

🎪

Antique & Classic Boat Show

Held each July, this beloved event draws wooden boat enthusiasts from across the Northeast for a weekend of stunning watercraft on the lake.

🍽️

Restaurants & Dining

For a village of 2,500 people, Skaneateles punches well above its weight in dining — ranging from beloved casual institutions to white-tablecloth lakeside experiences.

🦞

The Sherwood Inn

A Skaneateles landmark since 1807 — the original stagecoach inn founded by Isaac Sherwood. An elegant lakefront inn and dining room that remains one of the finest in the Finger Lakes. Classic American fare, an exceptional wine list, and views to match.

🐟

Doug's Fish Fry

A beloved local institution on Jordan Street. Cash-only, no-frills, and absolutely worth it — locals swear by the fish fry and the haddock sandwich. A must-visit counterpoint to the village's upscale options.

🍕

Mandana Inn

A short drive south — a rustic roadhouse that has been a local gathering spot for generations, known for pizza, cold beer, and an unpretentious atmosphere.

☕

Vermont Green Mountain Specialty Co.

The village's go-to coffee stop on East Genesee Street — ideal for a morning latte before a walk along the lake.

🍫

Chocolate & More

A downtown staple for handmade chocolates, truffles, and confections — a dangerous stop on any afternoon walk through the village.

🛍️

Shops & Local Businesses

Skaneateles' downtown along East Genesee Street is compact, curated, and notably independent — the village has resisted chain retail, keeping its core filled with boutiques, galleries, and local specialists.

Arts & Crafts

Papavero Clay Studio

Wheel-throwing and hand-building classes — a favorite creative outlet for residents and a great window into the village's artisan culture.

Antiques

Skaneateles Antique Center

Multi-dealer antique shop with an excellent selection of Americana, furniture, and regional collectibles — well worth an afternoon browse.

Clothing

The Wandering Kind

A popular women's boutique known for casual and resort-style apparel perfectly suited to lakeside life.

Books & Gallery

Skaneateles Library Gallery

The public library hosts a walk-in gallery of works by the late Skaneateles artist John Barrow — a quiet cultural gem in the heart of the village.

Home & Décor

Village Boutiques

Several home decoration and gift shops line the downtown, catering to second-home buyers and seasonal visitors seeking the Finger Lakes aesthetic.

Groceries

Wegmans — Auburn (6 mi)

The nearest full-service Wegmans is just 6 miles away in Auburn — a short drive for the full western New York grocery experience.

🏞️

Outdoor & Recreation

⛵

Skaneateles Lake — Boating, Swimming & Fishing

Sailing, kayaking, motor boating, and paddleboarding are central to summer life. Clift Park on the waterfront hosts live music on Friday nights — locals spread blankets on the lawn in front of the gazebo.

🥾

Charlie C. Major Nature Trail

A beloved local walking and biking trail following the Skaneateles Creek outlet — easy access to nature just steps from the village center.

🎈

Hot Air Balloon Rides

Several operators offer rides over the Finger Lakes from the Skaneateles area — one of the most dramatic ways to take in the lake and its surrounding hills.

⛷️

Ski Skan — Right in Skaneateles

The local ski area beloved by Skaneateles residents — a classic Central New York hill with a loyal community following for family skiing and weekend days on the slopes.

🍷

Finger Lakes Wine Trails

Skaneateles sits at the eastern edge of the Finger Lakes wine country — the Cayuga Lake Wine Trail is a short drive west, with dozens of wineries open year-round.

🏇

Polo at Skaneateles

Weekend polo matches in the summer — a beloved local tradition that speaks to the village's long history as a retreat for New York's well-heeled set.

Schools

A+

Skaneateles Central School District

Consistently rated one of the top school districts in Central New York and among the best in New York State. The district earns an overall A rating from Niche, with strengths in academics, college preparation, and extracurricular offerings. Small class sizes and strong parental involvement make it a primary driver of home values and one of the most cited reasons families choose Skaneateles over surrounding communities.

Real Estate Overview

Skaneateles is one of the tightest real estate markets in Central New York. Village homes range from $300K–$700K for inland properties and historic Victorians, while lakefront properties routinely climb into the millions. Over half of homes were built before 1939 — buyers find Federal, Greek Revival, Italianate, and Colonial Revival architecture throughout.
